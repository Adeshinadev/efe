ABOUT THE CONVENER

Owajoba Busayo is the CEO of De-Elegant Expertise Palais, a fashion house based in Ado-Ekiti, Ekiti state. Elegant as she is popularly called hails from Emure-Ekiti, Ekiti State. She studied Accounting at the prestigious Ekiti state University. In 2017, she launched the First ever fashion event "Ekiti Fashion Exhibition" in Ado-Ekiti, Ekiti state, under her fashion brand ''De Elegant Expertise Palais'' which featured over 10 fashion designers showcasing their designs. The second edition of the show which featured presentation of awards held in 2018 and it attracted numerous talented designers, models and fashion entrepreneurs within and outside the state. The event has since grown to become an iconic platform for fashion entrepreneurs to showcase their designs, wears and accessories in Ekiti during its exhibition & runway which has become an annual event and has had five edition so far. Owajoba Busayo was appointed as a member of the technical committee of EKIFEST 2019. She was also appointed as a member of the mobilizing committee for the Ekiti Entrepreneurship Week 2019. In 2020 and 2021 respectively she consulted as a Fashion Designer for Ministry of art and culture at Ekifest. She is also a member and the Assistant Project Supervisor of FADAN Ekiti (Fashion Designer Association of Nigeria) Apart from being passionate about showcasing the genius of Ekiti fashion entrepreneurs to the world, Owajoba Busayo is also committed to giving back to her society and acknowledging excellence. Hence, she organizes a yearly fashion based Skill acquisition program with rewards as well as presenting awards to outstanding personalities in the society and the fashion industry in Ekiti state respectively. Both events are hosted alongside her annual Ekiti Fashion Exhibition. In 2020 she was Awarded by the Are Afe Babalola Beneficiaries worldwide as the fashion designer of the year. Her most recent was the Ekiti future Award 2022 where she was recognized with an award as the fashion designer of the year.

ABOUT EKITI FASHION EXHIBITION (EFE)

With increasing number of youths plunging into depression and crime, there is no better time than now to empower the youths to become self-reliant and active ambassadors of Ekiti state. The fashion industry can be a pathway to eventuating such an agenda. Ekiti Fashion Exhibition is an annual event that aims to empower youths and expand the state's fashion industry through skill acquisition and fashion exhibition shows.  Running for 5 years now, the 6th edition is scheduled to hold in Ado-Ekiti this year. We are aware that a selfless vision as this would require the partnership with the state, organisations, individuals and cooperation of the people. To this end Ekiti Fashion Exhibition has deemed it fit to empower young people who have been equipped with fashion skills by empowering their brand with a start up plan. The winner of the Ekiti young Fashionprenuer contests was given a well equipped space and her rent was paid for. The first runner up was given a complete Electric/Manual Machine, while the second runner up was given a Mini Electric Machine.

Background

Youths in Ekiti State constitutes a part of the Nigeria youth unemployment figure that presently stand at 23.69%. This figure threatens the society as victims of this hazardous situation that has made a lot of young persons plunge into criminal activities. A good way to reduce this figure as well as curb the menace that comes with it is to empower the youths through skill acquisition. Youth empowerment comes with no cons: but it benefits both the youths and host state. In other words, youth empowerment are greatly beneficial to societies that takes it seriously and do it rightly.  De-Elegant Expertise Palais (DEEP)--a fashion enterprise based in Ekiti state has in the last five years committed its strength not only to the business of fashion but to empowering youths of Ekiti state through its annual event, EKITI FASHION EXHIBITION. We do this convinced of the immense benefit of fashion to youths and state. Empowering youth in fashion would make them self-employed; thus, help them to be less dependent on the state for sustenance. The state also can explore this area of untapped area in her pursuit to expand the economy. We have examples of cities across the world who have engaged the riches of the fashion industry and have grown their economy in so doing. Paris is a city as such: having fashion contributing 2.7% share to her GDP and created jobs for over 1million individuals.
